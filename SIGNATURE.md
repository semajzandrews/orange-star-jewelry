# Orange Star Jewelry — SIGNATURE

**Concept:** "At the Bench." Orange Star is not a glass-case discount counter on
Main Street — it is a working goldsmith's atelier (master craftsman Rahim,
custom work since 1997, customers of 20+ years). The site is built around the
heat and craft of the bench: molten gold, the torch, the hand that sets a stone.

**Signature moment:** A full-bleed cinematic hero where an oversized Clash
Display wordmark sits over a slow Ken-Burns push on a goldsmith's-bench photo,
with a "molten edge" — a brick-red (#B53B2B) to ember light-bloom that breathes
behind the headline like heat off a torch. A thin gold seam draws itself across
the hero as you arrive. It says "real hands, real fire, real gold," which is the
brand truth (custom + repair by a named master), and it looks nothing like the
neighbor discount jewelers or like our velvet-museum Edgar build.

**Lane (locked):** accent #B53B2B · Clash Display (Fontshare) · full-bleed
cinematic hero, oversized headline · mood BOLD.

**Palette:** near-black forge ground (#140F0D), warm bone (#F2E9DD), brick-ember
accent #B53B2B, true gold leaf (#C8943B) used sparingly as the metal, ash-grey
rules. Warm, hot, hand-made — not cold luxury.

**Type:** Clash Display (display, oversized) + a clean grotesque body
(General Sans / Satoshi via Fontshare) + tabular nums for hours/specs.

**Motion:** Lenis smooth scroll, GSAP/Motion whileInView rises, hero Ken-Burns +
heat-bloom, a single drawn gold seam. No scroll-snap, no gimmicks.

**Why it diverges from Edgar (sibling jeweler, BURNED):** Edgar = velvet
aubergine, Bodoni Moda, champagne gold, a tumbling diamond, museum hush. Orange
Star = forge black + brick-red, Clash Display, gold-as-metal, the bench and the
torch, bold heat. Opposite hue family, opposite type class, opposite mood.

**Signature line:** "On Main Street since 1997, gold takes its shape by hand at the Orange Star bench."
